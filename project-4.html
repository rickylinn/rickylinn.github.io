Project Overview

The UMC – Machine Monitoring System was developed to provide a flexible and scalable condition monitoring solution for a large industrial plant where conventional cabling installation was challenging and costly. To overcome these site constraints, a wireless LoRaWAN-based architecture was proposed and implemented, utilizing LoRaWAN sensors and gateways for long-range, low-power industrial monitoring applications.

The system monitors critical machine conditions such as abnormal vibration, equipment health status, and water/oil leakage detection, enabling early fault identification and predictive maintenance capabilities. All monitoring data and alarm statuses are transmitted wirelessly through the LoRaWAN network and integrated into the existing plant SCADA system via LoRaWAN Gateway, providing operators with centralized real-time visibility, alarm management, and historical trending.

As the control system vendor, I was responsible for the overall monitoring system design, communication integration, testing & commissioning (T&C), and operator training. The solution significantly reduced installation complexity, minimized downtime during deployment, and enhanced plant operational reliability through intelligent wireless monitoring technology.
